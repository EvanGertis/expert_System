CSCI 1301 Programming Principles I [O]
4 Credit Hours. 3 Lecture Hours. 2 Lab Hours.
Provides a fundamental understanding of the development of computer solutions to solve problems with emphasis on
structured, top-down development and testing. Concepts include the following: an overview of computer system
design, problem solving and procedural abstraction design of computer solutions, algorithm development using simple
data types and control structures, implementation and testing of programmed problem solutions, design modularization
using subprograms and structured and user-defined data types.
Prerequisite(s): A minimum grade of "C" in MATH 1441.
